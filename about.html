About the Practitioner Liên Zayhowski

Career

Liên started her private practices in Lowell and later on in Woburn, where she treated patients with chronic pain, sports injuries, fibromyalgia, allergies, insomnia, infertility and women’s health, stress, neurological and digestive issues. In 2012 and 2013 her private practice, Sunrise Acupuncture, was voted “Greater Woburn’s Best Acupuncture Therapy.” Since 2011, she’s also been working at a major pain center in the North Shore with a team of doctors, nurse practitioners, chiropractors, and physical and massage therapists, to treat chronic and severe pain cases. Liên closed her private practice in 2014 to work at Massachusetts General Hospital (MGH Boston) treating patients in Oncology, at Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center Cheng-Tsui Integrated Health Center (BIDMC Longwood and Chestnut Hill locations), as well as the pain center in the North Shore. Liên re-opens her private practice in the fall of 2018 because she enjoys giving personalized attention to her patients and treating one patient for the whole duration of the patient’s acupuncture session.


Education

Liên received a Master’s degree in Acupuncture and Oriental Medicine (MAOM) in 2007 from the New England School of Acupunture (MAOM). She went on to study Functional Medicine, Orthopedic Acupuncture, Dr. Tan’s Global Balance and Balancing Methods, Master Tung’s Points, Foot Reflexology, Tuina, non-insertive methods such as ShakuJu and Biomagnetic Acupoint Therapy, Cancer and Oriental Medicine, and Herbal Medicine for Women’s Health. In 2013, she spent ten days climbing the Emei Mountain in China, learning Emei Qigong and Tuina, meditating, and experiencing traditional Chinese culture.


For her own well-being, Liên implements natural healing through foods and natural supplements, meditation, yoga, acupuncture, massage therapy, chiropractic care, and physical therapy.
